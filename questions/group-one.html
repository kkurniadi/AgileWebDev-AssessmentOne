Content Definitions (Group One)

Analysis is the process of investigating a problem, as well as the requirements said problem imposes on a system.
Design is the process of finding a conceptual solution that fulfils the requirements, which can then be implemented.

Architectural requirements are the requirements involved in creating a website layout that satisfies the user or businessâ€™s needs.
They define the components that make up a website and services each of these components will provide as a whole.
These requirements may have many forms, such as technical constraints, functional aspects, visual appearance and security parameters.

Website design structures are the structural projection of information in a space that provides intuitive content access.
They are an important part of developing page navigation.
These structures may fall into one of four different types, those being hierarchical, sequential, matrix or database models.

User interface design requirements and production processes are a methodology that will allow you to polish your user interfaces to best fit the business.
They include product definition, research, analysis, design and validation or testing.
